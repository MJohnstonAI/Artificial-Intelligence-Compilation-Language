## Hacker News

Title: AICL: an AI-native semantic compilation research spec

Post:

I am sharing AICL, the Artificial Intelligence Compilation Language, as a pre-implementation research specification.

The goal is not to build a nicer Python, a Rust competitor, or another human-first DSL. AICL is trying to define the semantic authority layer above ordinary code for AI systems that generate, verify, repair, and materialize software.

The core artifacts are semantic contracts, ICC, SHG, WKG-grounded meaning, proof obligations, policy/capability boundaries, provenance, and materialization outputs. Python, TypeScript, Kotlin, Swift, SQL, Rust, and other languages may still be output targets.

The most useful review is not "I prefer this syntax." The requested review method is to run a strong AI model against the repository and submit structured contradictions, weak semantics, missing proof obligations, overclaims, and minimal fixes.

## r/ProgrammingLanguages

Title: AICL: pre-implementation research spec for AI-native semantic compilation

Post:

AICL is a research specification for an AI-native semantic compilation layer, not a finished compiler and not a human-first language competing with Python/Rust/TypeScript at the syntax layer.

The design premise is that AI-built software needs first-class artifacts for intent, policy, capability containment, proof obligations, resource budgets, provenance, WKG-grounded meaning, SHG planning, and materialization contracts. Human-readable syntax may exist as a projection layer, but it is not the root authority.

I am especially interested in critique of type authority, proof tiers, grammar/IR separation, operational semantics, compile/runtime boundaries, and places where the repo accidentally invites legacy-language misreadings.

Preferred review method: run a strong AI model against the evaluation prompt in the repo and submit a structured contradiction report or challenge brief.
